Chicken Stir Fry

A classic and easy-to-make dish

Chicken stir fry is a delicious and quick meal. Featuring pan fried chicken mixed with vegetables and covered in a special hand-made stir-fry sauce this dish is both easy to prepare and quick to cook.

Great for a family dinner, social potluck, or even meal prepping for a week's worth of lunches, chicken stir fry is one of my favourite dishes to make.

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lbs. boneless chicken breast
  • 1 large white onion
  • 2 large bell peppers
  • 1 lb. broccoli
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch of ginger
  • 1 3/4 cups of low-sodium chicken stock
  • 1/4 cups soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp. honey
  • 1 tbsp. rice vinegar
  • 3 tbsp. cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp. sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil

Steps

  1. Slice the onion, bell peppers, and chicken into thin slices, and chop the brocolli into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Mice the garlic and ginger.
  3. In a medium bowl, add chicken stock, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, cornstarch, garlic, and ginger. Whisk together.
  4. Preheat 1 tablespoon in a large skillet or wok on medium heat. Place sliced chicken into the pan/wok. Cook unconvered for 3 minutes per side stirring sparingly
  5. Remove chicken from pan and set it aside. Set the heat to high and add 1 more tablespoon of olive oil. Add sliced onions and bell peppers to the pan. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring sparingly.
  6. Return heat to medium; remove the onions and bell peppers from the pan and place them to the side.
  7. Add the brocolli to the pan, whisk sauce once again to ensure no cornstarch is stuck to the bottom of the bowl. After whisking, add the sauce to the pan.
  8. Let the sauce come to a boil, stirring constantly. Cook until the sauce is thickened.
  9. Once the sauce is thickened, return the cooked chicken, onions, and bell peppers to the pan, mixing them into the sauce. Turn off the heat
  10. Garnish with sesame seeds, and serve alone or over rice.
