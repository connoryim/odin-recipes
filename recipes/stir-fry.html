Chicken Stir Fry

A classic and easy to make dish

Chicken stir fry is a delicious and quick meal. Featuring pan friend chicken mixed with vegetables and covered in a special hand made stir fry sauce. This dish is both easy to prepare and quick to cook.

Great for a family night, social potluck, or even meal prepping for a week's worth of lunches; chicken stir fry is one of my favourite dishes to make.

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lbs boneless chicken breast
  • 1 large white onion
  • 2 large bell peppers
  • 1 lb broccoli
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 1 3/4 cup low sodium chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Steps

  1. Slice the onion, bell peppers, and chicken into thin slices and chop brocolli into bitsized pieces
  2. Mice the garlic and ginger
  3. In a medium bowl add chicken stock, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar cornstarch, garlic, and ginger. Whisk together
  4. Preheat 1 tbsp in a large skillet or wok on medium heat, place sliced chicken into the pan/wok. Cook unconvered for 3 minutes per side stirring sparingly
  5. Remove chicken from pan and place to the side. Set heat to high and add 1 more tbsp of oil. Add sliced onions and bell peppers to pan. Cook for 2 minutes stirring sparingly
  6. Return heat to medium, remove onions and bell peppers from pan and place them to the side.
  7. add brocolli to pan/wok, whisk sauce once again to ensure no cornstarch is stuck onto the bottom of the bowl. Aftering whisking add contents to pan
  8. Let sauce come to a boil stirring constantly. Cook until the sauce is thickened.
  9. Once sauce is thickened return cooked chicken, onions, and bell peppers to pan mixing them into the sauce. Turn off the heat
  10. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve alone or over rice
